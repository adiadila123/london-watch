How it works & FAQ

Information guidelines on how to report issues and who addresses them.

Reporting Guide: How do I submit a report correctly?

Submitting a detailed report helps local teams resolve the issue more quickly:

  • Precise location: Place the red pin exactly on the map. You can tap the map or drag the existing pin to fine-tune its position.
  • Add a photograph: A clear image shows the extent of the problem (e.g. the depth of a pothole or the size of a fly-tip). The app automatically compresses large photos before uploading.
  • Respect privacy: When taking photos, try to avoid capturing people's faces or vehicle registration plates.
  • Brief description: Add useful details, for example: "Deep pothole in lane 1, directly outside number 42".
TfL vs. Local Council: Who is responsible?

In London, responsibility for infrastructure is shared between Transport for London (TfL) and Borough Councils:

Authority Responsibilities
Transport for London (TfL)
  • Red Routes (e.g. A1, A406, Euston Road).
  • Tube stations and their immediate surroundings.
  • Bus stops and shelters.
  • Traffic lights across the capital.
Local Council (Borough Council)
  • Residential streets and their pavements.
  • Street lighting on secondary roads.
  • Parks, local green spaces, and playgrounds.
  • Fly-tipping and street litter bins.
Useful tip: When you submit a report in the app, it is logged in the platform's open database, allowing the relevant authority to pick up the incident based on the GPS coordinates provided.
What are Confirmations?

Confirmations represent community endorsement of a report:

  • If you spot an issue on the map that has already been reported by someone else, there is no need to submit a duplicate report.
  • Open the pin on the map and press the "Confirm" button. This signals to the council that the problem is still active and affects multiple residents.
  • Reports with a high number of confirmations are highlighted in the admin feed to be prioritised in repair schedules.
How does issue resolution work, and how do I become an admin?

All submitted reports appear in the public feed and on the map as "Reported".

  • Status updates: Local council inspectors and platform administrators can change the status of reports to "In Progress" (when a team has been dispatched) and "Resolved" (once works are complete).
  • Admin access: The admin panel (admin.html) is secured via Supabase and is reserved exclusively for authorised council representatives, neighbourhood co-ordinators, and maintenance partners.
